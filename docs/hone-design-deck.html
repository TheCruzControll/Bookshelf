Hone design direction

Warm, hand drawn, wabi-sabi, never cold.

A tactile social reading app for books and taste. The interface should feel like paper, ink, folk textile, and a friend's shelf.

Naming idea

Hon + hone

Hon nods to the Japanese word for book. Hone means to sharpen. The product promise is simple: use books and trusted readers to sharpen your taste.

Brand sentence

Hone is a social shelf where friends rank what they read, so your next book comes from taste you trust.

Color source

Sanzo Wada as the palette spine.

The palette borrows the spirit of A Dictionary of Color Combinations: muted harmony, warm material colors, and deliberate accents rather than bright app-primary color.

Visual inspiration

Japanese folk art, not generic beige.

The influence from Samiro Yunoki points toward stencil-like repetition, bold simplified shapes, imperfect geometry, and color that feels dyed rather than backlit.

Interface rules

Minimal, but with fingerprints.

Paper first

No white backgrounds. Cream and ivory bases mimic washi paper.

Imperfect edges

Uneven radii, ink borders, and small rotations keep the UI human.

Accent with restraint

Red, green, indigo, and mineral blue appear as folk-art accents.
